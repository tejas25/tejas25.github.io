About

I am currently pursuing M.S. in Urban Informatics degree from Northeastern University where the focus is on the big questions faced by cities in the 21st century with the help of Data Science. I got intrested in data science during my PhD where, I got the glimps of handaling and analysisng somewhat large dataset and potential of DATA SCIENCE to extract knowledge or insight from data in various forms and translate it into action.

Before this I have worked at Ernst and Young LLP in Ahmedabad, India, where I was involved on various projects relating to policy planning and investment promotion for Government of Gujarat State, India.

I have graduated with Master of Urban and Rural Planning (IIT Roorkee) and B.Tech Civil Engineering (CEPT University), prior to my job at EY. and have done my PhD research on Regional Transportation Policy Planning from Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee. The study focused on how transportation can help and improve the sustainable development of the region and what kind of policies that can be adopted to speedup this process. I had used System Dynamics Modeling technique to come up with optimum strategies for transportation policy planning. You would like to see my publications through this link.

I was fortunate being selected as promising researcher, among 24 other researchers globally, as a part of prestigious Young Scientist Summer Program (YSSP 2014-15) held in South Africa, by International Institute of Applied System Analysis (IIASA Austria) and National Research Foundation (NRF South Africa). You would like to see my contribution through this link.

I’m ready to take on the challenges that face modern cities today. I’m particularly interested in work involving quantitative analysis (with the use of predictive modeling and machine learning techniques) and data visualization.
